Simspanion

The Sims Gallery, a heavily used feature of the Sims community, is one of the hardest things to access outside of the player's console of choice. Simspanion aims to create a smaller, fully functional version of the Gallery that can be used on player's mobile devices, as well as provide a place for players to view the latest news and patch notes for The Sims 4.

My Process

Research

To understand what a true companion app would need, I researched competitor apps and the source material to find essential features.

I conducted research on RDR2, the Red Dead Redemption 2 companion app, and Xbox, the Xbox companion app, and compared their features to Simspanion.

Ideation

Once research was done, low-fidelity wireframes were created based on the information gathered and the user flows I created.

Wireframes were first drafted on paper to keep things fast and flexible.

Design

Designing for Simspanion presented an interesting challenge-- how do I figure out how to condense the existing UI while retaining functionality? I needed to keep in mind the native restraints of both iOS and Android while following EA branding guidelines.

Prototype

Two prototypes were used to conduct usability testing for both the iOS product and the Android product.

View iOS Prototype View Android Prototype

Reflection & Conclusion

Trying to imitate EA's branding was by far the most challenging part of this project. The original desktop version of the Gallery is a bit clunky and hard to work with, so I had to find a way to redesign the website and shrink it all while staying true to the original feel of the Gallery.

In the end, I decided to mix the UI of the in-game gallery and the EA website, making something more accessible but faithful to longtime Sims fans. The in-game Gallery also has more features than its desktop counterpart, so I included many of the missing features, such as a more robust filtering system.
